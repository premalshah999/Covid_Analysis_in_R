Policy Timing Optimization

When Should Governments Act to Prevent Healthcare System Collapse?

97
Regions Analyzed

The Challenge

Can we predict ICU surges 14 days in advance to give policymakers time to act?

Approach

  • COVID-19 Data Hub (97 regions)
  • Target: ICU surge >25% in 14 days
  • Random Forest vs Logistic Regression

What predicts surges better—current ICU load or policy stringency?

Data Pipeline

01 — Data Source

COVID-19 Data Hub: ICU metrics, case counts, policy stringency indices

02 — Cleaning

Forward-fill imputation, 7-day rolling averages, outlier removal

03 — Feature Engineering

ICU per 100k, growth rates, stringency lags (7, 14, 21 days)

Model Performance

Predictive Accuracy (AUC)

0.5 = random, 1.0 = perfect

Logistic Regression 0.773
Random Forest 0.964

Random Forest achieves 96.4% accuracy by capturing non-linear relationships.

Key Findings

02

03
Ceiling Effect

High ICU: 4.9% risk vs Low ICU: 26.5% risk

04
Modest Policy Effect

High stringency: 22.2% vs Lower: 24.9-25.6%

Impact & Takeaways

What We Achieved

  • 96.4% accuracy predicting surges 14 days ahead
  • Identified current ICU load as #1 predictor
  • Discovered counterintuitive ceiling effect
  • Quantified modest policy-surge associations

Policy Implications

  • Monitor ICU utilization directly over case counts
  • Use ML for proactive 14-day warnings
  • Interpret high-ICU "safety" with caution
  • Consider observational data limitations

Data-Driven Pandemic Response

Predictive models enable proactive intervention without relying solely on reactive policy stringency measures.
